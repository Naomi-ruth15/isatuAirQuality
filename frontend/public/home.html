About

The ISAT U Ambiance Project develops and deploys indoor air quality (IAQ) monitoring devices in select ISAT University buildings. These devices continuously monitor key ambient parameters: TVOC, eCO2, humidity, temperature, and dust density. A real-time dashboard displays this data, calculating and presenting an Indoor Air Quality Index (IAQI) to reflect the current air quality status. This allows prompt investigation of environmental concerns, ultimately improving student health and well-being.

Framework, Monitoring Index & Research Partnership

Framework

We aim to develop a sustainable and adaptive framework based on IAQ data, building use, and green building codes. This guides optimal design and retrofitting for air quality and well-being.

Monitoring Index

The IAQ Monitoring Index integrates real-time sensor data and AI to assess indoor air. User feedback enhances accuracy and informs immediate and long-term actions.

Research Partnership

We consult and coordinate with SUCs to develop monitoring databases and IAQ indexing, aligning with UNSD goals for sustainability.
